YIELD (2022)

Situated on Pacific Park overlooking Newcastle Beach, Yield is a project that intends to challenge the functions of public architecture by blurring the lines between private domestic spaces and open public spaces.

The project comprises of prefabricated concrete hexagonal columns extending from a sloped site, mimicking the appearance of a little forest. The bases of the hexagonal columns sit on hexagonal brick pavers that cascade down the site, allowing for a variety of spaces to be configured within the project.

The slope naturally creates an opportunity for prospect and refuge, with the rear end of the project providing a sheltered, “private” space for “domestic” activities such as cooking, cleaning and resting. As users approach the front end of the site, the project gradually appears more open, transitioning itself into a “public” space for communal gathering and entertaining on a wider scale.
